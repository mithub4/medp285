Ataulfo

The 'Ataúlfo' mango is a mango cultivar from Mexico. Ataulfo mangos are golden yellow and generally weigh between 6 and 10 ounces (170 and 280 g), with a somewhat sigmoid (oblong) shape and a gold-yellow skin. The flesh is not Fibrous , and the pit is thin. They were named for grower Ataulfo Morales Gordillo. Since August 27, 2003, the Ataulfo mango is one of the 18 Mexican Designations of Origin.

Haden

The 'Haden' mango is a historically important cultivar that played a key role in shaping global mango cultivation. Known for its vibrant red-yellow skin and sweet, aromatic flavor, it offers a juicy texture with subtle floral and spicy notes. Its enduring legacy continues through its influence on modern mango breeding and its popularity in both fresh and culinary uses.

Kesar

The Kesar mango was first cultivated in 1931 by Junagadh Wazir Sale Bhai and gained its name from the Nawab of Junagadh, inspired by its saffron-colored pulp. It is India’s second-most exported mango variety and is prized for its rich, sweet flavor. Though native to Gujarat, it also grows well in climates like South Florida.

Alphonso

The Alphonso mango is named after Afonso de Albuquerque, a Portuguese viceroy, and was developed through grafting techniques introduced by Jesuit missionaries in Goa. By the mid-1500s, Alphonsos were cultivated in Portuguese Bombay and presented to officials in Goa. Known for its rich flavor and smooth texture, it is one of the most expensive mango varieties and is primarily grown in the Konkan region and parts of South Gujarat.

Imam Pasand

The Imam Pasand mango, primarily grown in Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, and Tamil Nadu, is celebrated for its rich flavor and royal heritage. Large and fiberless with juicy, sweet flesh, it offers complex notes of honey, citrus, and floral aromas. Often called the "King of Mangoes," it was once favored by Indian royalty and is available during the mid-season from late April to early June.

Jakarta

The Jakarta mango, developed in South Florida by Laurence Zill, is believed to be a hybrid of the 'Bombay' and 'Kent' varieties. It produces medium-to-large, fiberless fruit with a rich, sweet flavor and a distinctive resinous aroma. Though not widely commercialized, it remains a favorite among mango enthusiasts and is grown in Florida home gardens.

Julie

The Julie mango, introduced to the U.S. in the 1910s by Lawrence Zill, originates from the West Indies, particularly Trinidad and Tobago. Known for its rich, sweet flavor and vibrant tropical aroma, it is a small to medium-sized fruit with a yellow-orange skin and deep orange, fiberless flesh. Its compact tree makes it ideal for home gardens, though it requires care to prevent fungal diseases like Anthracnose.

Nam Doc Mai Mun

The DE Nam Dok Mai Mun is a new, highly sought-after variety of the Nam Dok Mai Mun family, originating in Thailand. It has an elongated shape with a pointed tip and wide shoulders, with large fruits weighing over 1500 grams, featuring a crunchy, fiberless texture and a distinct, sweet flavor. It is recommended to be eaten both when green or yellow-ripe.

Francis

The Madame Francis Mango is a beloved dessert variety from Haiti, often considered the country's "de facto" mango, similar to Alphonso in India. It has a bright green and yellow skin, with a dark orange, fibrous flesh that is soft, juicy, and rich in sweet, spicy flavor. Known for ripening early in the season, it frequently produces multiple crops in Florida and tropical regions.
